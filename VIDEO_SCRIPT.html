1. THE PROBLEM — Why $10M in USDT Is Different

The Tax Problem

USDT is a stablecoin pegged to the dollar. 1 USDT ≈ $1. But the moment you sell it on Coinbase, Kraken, or any exchange — that's a taxable disposal in the eyes of the IRS.

ScenarioTax Hit on $10M
Sell all at once (short-term)$3.7M (37% ordinary income rate)
Sell all at once (long-term)$2.38M (20% LTCG + 3.8% NIIT)
Borrow against it$0 (not a taxable event)
Spend it directly$0 (not a taxable event)
Lend it for revenue share$0 (income on the return, not the principal)

The KYC Problem

Every major exchange requires full government ID, selfie, proof of address, and bank account verification. Your financial life is on record with the IRS and the exchange.

The Core Insight

"The goal isn't to sell your USDT. The goal is to access the value it represents — without ever hitting a taxable event."

The IRS treats crypto as property. Selling = disposal = capital gains. But borrowing against it? Not taxable. Lending it? Not taxable. Spending it? Not taxable.

7. FRAUD CASES — How They Got Caught

Case 1: Crypto Dispensers (Nov 2025) — $10M

What: Chicago-based cash-to-BTC ATMs. Founder: Firas Isa.

Charged: $10 million money laundering conspiracy. Max: 20 years federal prison.

How caught: Chainalysis traced funds through ATMs. Banks flagged suspicious cash deposits. FBI followed money from fraud victims → through the ATMs → to his accounts.

Lesson: Every ATM transaction is on-chain. Every cash deposit is tracked.

Case 2: Paxful (Dec 2025) — $7.5M Fine + Shutdown

What: One of the world's largest P2P platforms. 14M users. 140+ countries.

Charged: Unlicensed money transmitting, BSA violations, money laundering conspiracy.

How caught: No MTL licenses in multiple states. No KYC/AML program. Willful blindness — knew scammers used platform, did nothing. No SAR filings.

Lesson: You're responsible for what happens on your platform.

Case 3: Samourai Wallet (2025)

What: Privacy wallet that mixed transactions.

Charged: $100M+ money laundering.

Sentence: CEO: 60 months prison. CPO: 48 months prison.

Lesson: Privacy mixing services = explicit money laundering intent in the eyes of the law.

Case 4: Tether Freezes (2025–2026) — $700M+ Frozen

What: Tether (USDT issuer) froze funds connected to fraud.

Amounts: $344M, $38M, $61M, $225M seized across multiple actions.

Lesson: Tether can freeze your USDT instantly. No warning. No appeal.
